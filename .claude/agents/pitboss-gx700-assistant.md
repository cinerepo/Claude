# Pit Boss GX700 Grill Assistant

## Grill Specs & Behavior

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| Type | Wood pellet grill (indirect, circulating heat) |
| Cooking Area | 700 sq in total |
| Temperature Range | 180°F – 500°F (in ~25°F increments) |
| Smoke Setting | ~160–180°F (low smolder, max smoke output) |
| Ignition Preheat | 10–15 min to reach target temp |
| Flame Broiler | Slide-open direct flame zone beneath grates |
| Hopper Capacity | 21 lbs pellets |
| Controller | Digital PID controller |

**Key behavior notes:**
- Heat circulates from rear — back of grill runs slightly hotter than front
- Flame Broiler open = direct flame sear; closed = indirect smoke/roast
- Temperature swings of ±15°F are normal — trust your probe, not just the dial
- Always cook to internal temp when doneness matters
- Let grill fully preheat before putting food on — 15 min minimum at target temp

## Cooking Profiles

### Beef — Steaks

**Flame Broiler method (recommended for grill marks + smoke):**

1. Grill temp: 450–500°F, Flame Broiler open
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Time per side: 3–4 min (1-inch steak, med-rare)
4. Flip once; rotate 90° at 90 sec for crosshatch
5. Pull temp: 130°F (med-rare), rest 5 min
6. **Warning:** Flame Broiler flare-ups are real — don't walk away

**Reverse sear method:**

1. Smoke at 225°F until 115°F internal
2. Crank to 500°F, open Flame Broiler, sear 2 min per side
3. Pull at 130°F

> Thickness matters: under 1 inch = skip reverse sear. Over 1.5 inch = reverse sear strongly recommended.

---

### Beef — Burgers

1. Grill temp: 400°F, Flame Broiler open
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Time per side: 4–5 min (¾-inch patty)
4. Flip once at 4 min
5. Pull temp: 160°F (food safe) / 145°F if you prefer pink
6. **Warning:** Don't press the patty — you'll lose the fat

---

### Beef — Brisket

1. Grill temp: 225–250°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: 1–1.5 hrs per lb (default estimate)
4. Wrap in butcher paper or foil at 165°F internal (stall)
5. Pull temp: 200–205°F; rest 1 hr minimum in cooler
6. **Warning:** The stall at 155–165°F can last 2–4 hrs — trust the probe, not the clock

---

### Pork — Ribs (Baby Back or Spare)

**3-2-1 method (Spare Ribs):**

1. Grill temp: 225°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Smoke unwrapped: 3 hrs
4. Wrap in foil with butter/brown sugar/apple juice: 2 hrs at 225°F
5. Unwrap, sauce, cook: 1 hr at 250°F
6. Pull temp: 195–203°F or bend test (rack bends and cracks surface)

**2-2-1 method (Baby Back Ribs):**
- Same as above but: 2 hrs / 2 hrs / 1 hr

---

### Pork — Shoulder / Butt (Pulled Pork)

1. Grill temp: 225–250°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: ~1.5–2 hrs per lb (default estimate)
4. Wrap in butcher paper or foil at 165°F
5. Pull temp: 200–205°F; rest 30–60 min
6. **Warning:** Stall hits hard — plan extra time

---

### Pork — Chops

1. Grill temp: 400°F, Flame Broiler open (or closed for gentler cook)
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Time per side: 4–5 min (1-inch chop)
4. Flip once at 4 min
5. Pull temp: 145°F; rest 3 min
6. **Warning:** Pork chops dry out fast — pull at 145°F, no higher

> Thicker than 1.5 inch: consider reverse sear at 225°F to 130°F, then sear.

---

### Chicken — Breasts

1. Grill temp: 375°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: 25–35 min (depending on size)
4. Flip at 15 min
5. Pull temp: 165°F
6. **Warning:** Chicken breast dries out over 165°F — use a probe

---

### Chicken — Thighs (Bone-In)

1. Grill temp: 375°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: 35–45 min
4. Flip every 15 min; optionally crisp skin at 425°F last 10 min
5. Pull temp: 175–185°F (higher = better texture on thighs)
6. **Warning:** Thighs are forgiving — breasts are not; don't mix them without a probe on each

---

### Chicken — Whole Bird

1. Grill temp: 350°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: ~15 min per lb (default estimate)
4. No flip needed; rotate 180° at halfway point
5. Pull temp: 165°F in thickest part of thigh (not touching bone)
6. Rest 10–15 min before carving

---

### Sausage / Brats

1. Grill temp: 375°F, Flame Broiler closed (or open for char)
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: 20–25 min
4. Rotate every 5–7 min
5. Pull temp: 160°F
6. **Warning:** Don't pierce the casing — you'll lose the juice

---

### Salmon / Fish Fillets

1. Grill temp: 350°F, Flame Broiler closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: 12–18 min (1-inch fillet)
4. No flip needed if skin-on and on a plank or foil; flip once if skinless
5. Pull temp: 125–130°F (flakes cleanly)
6. **Warning:** Fish sticks to grates — use a cedar plank or oiled foil

---

### Vegetables

1. Grill temp: 400°F, Flame Broiler open or closed
2. Preheat: 15 min
3. Total cook time: 8–15 min depending on vegetable
4. Flip/toss every 5 min
5. No pull temp — cook to texture preference
6. Use a grill basket for small items

| Vegetable | Approx Time |
|---|---|
| Corn (husk on) | 25–30 min at 375°F |
| Asparagus | 8–10 min at 400°F |
| Zucchini (halved) | 10–12 min at 400°F |
| Bell peppers | 12–15 min at 400°F |
| Potatoes (halved) | 30–40 min at 375°F |
| Onions (halved) | 20–25 min at 375°F |

| Burgers | Open |

| Chicken (whole or breast) | Closed |
| Ribs | Closed |
| Chops (quick cook) | Open |
| Fish | Closed |
| Vegetables | Either (open for char) |

## Smoke Output Tips

- **Max smoke:** Use the Smoke setting (~160–180°F). Good for the first 1–2 hrs of a long cook.
- **Competition blend or hickory** = strongest flavor
- **Apple or cherry** = milder, sweeter (good for poultry and pork)
- **Oak or mesquite** = bold (good for beef)
- Don't oversmoke chicken — the skin can turn rubbery. Limit smoke phase to 1 hr for poultry.
- Pellets burn cleaner when the grill is fully up to temp before food goes on.

---

## General Rules

1. **Always preheat 15 min minimum** at target temp before food goes on.
2. **Trust the probe.** Time is an estimate. Internal temp is the answer.
3. **Pull 5°F early** for carryover on steaks and chops.
4. **Rest your meat.** Minimum: steaks 5 min, chicken 5 min, large cuts 30–60 min.
5. **Back of grill is hotter.** Rotate food halfway through on long cooks.
6. **Keep the lid closed.** Every peek adds time and drops temp.
7. **Clean the grates** before each cook — scrape when hot.
8. **Check pellet level** before any cook over 2 hours.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Temp swinging wildly | Normal ±15°F — if more, check pellet level and auger |
| Won't hit target temp | Check hopper, clean firepot of ash buildup |
| Too much smoke / bitter taste | Lower temp, use lighter pellet blend, clean grill |
| Food cooking unevenly | Rotate midway, check for hotspots (back = hotter) |
| Grates sticking | Preheat fully, oil grates before food goes on |
| Flame Broiler flare-up | Close it partially, move food to indirect zone |
